OT-62 TOPAS
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : The OT-62 TOPAS entered service with the Indian Army in the early 1970s, imported to bolster India’s mechanized infantry

    capability, particularly in amphibious operations.


  • Mjaor Operation : The OT-62 was deployed during the1971 Indo-Pak War,where its amphibious capabilities were especially useful in the riverine

    regions of East Pakistan (now Bangladesh). Its ability to transport troops across rivers and other water bodies made it ideal for this theater of war.


  • Retirement: The OT-62 began to be phased out in the 1990s as the Indian Army upgraded its mechanized fleet with newer APCs

    and infantry fighting vehicles (IFVs) like the BMP-2, which offered superior protection, firepower, and mobility.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 14.5 tons.

  • Engine : Powered by a Tatra T-928-14 V8 diesel engineproducing 180 horsepower.

  • Speed : Maximum speed of 60 km/h on-road, with amphibious capabilities allowing a water speed of up to 9 km/h.

  • Armament :
  • Primary : A 7.62mm PKT or 7.62mm SGMT machine gun mounted on a pintle.
  • Secondary : Some variants could mount additional machine guns or small-caliber cannons for enhanced support.

  • Armor : Welded steel armor offering protection against small arms fire and shell fragments, though vulnerable to anti-tank weapons.

  • Range : Operational range of approximately 500 km on land.

  • Crew : Operated by a crew of two (driver and commander) with the capacity to carry up to 18 infantry soldiers.

Historical Significance


The OT-62 TOPAS brought significant amphibious capabilities to the Indian Army’s mechanized infantry,

allowing for seamless transport of troops over water obstacles. This proved crucial in the 1971 Indo-Pak War

particularly in East Pakistan’s riverine terrain. The TOPAS marked a step forward in the mechanization of the

Indian Army’s infantry units, enhancing troop mobility in diverse terrain conditions.


Notable Missions


  • 1971 Indo-Pak War: The OT-62 TOPAS was widely used in the eastern front, where its amphibious capabilities were a game-changer in the river-laden terrain of East Pakistan. It allowed Indian forces to rapidly move troops across rivers and engage in swift, coordinated assaults, contributing to the swift victory in the eastern theater.

  • Amphibious Exercises and Counter-Insurgency:The OT-62 continued to be used in various amphibious training exercises, as well as in limited

    roles in internal security and counter-insurgency operations due to its troop-carrying capacity and mobility.
