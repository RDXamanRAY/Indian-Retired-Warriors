MIKOYAN-GUREVICH MiG-27
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : The MiG-27 entered service with the Indian Air Force in 1985. India acquired

    the aircraft to enhance its tactical strike capabilities and later produced the MiG-27 under license at Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL).


  • Mjaor Operation : The MiG-27 saw extensive action during the Kargil War of 1999, where it was instrumental in high-altitude strike

    missions. Its ground-attack capabilities made it well-suited for precision bombing in mountainous terrain. The aircraft was also deployed during various peacetime border patrols and for maintaining deterrence along India’s borders.


  • Retirement: The IAF retired the MiG-27 from active service in 2019, marking the end of an era. Although capable,

    the aircraft was becoming outdated and was replaced by modern multi-role fighters like the Sukhoi Su-30MKI and Mirage 2000.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 11 tons (empty weight).

  • Engine : Equipped with a Tumansky R-29B-300 turbojet engine, delivering 18,300 pounds of thrust.

  • Speed : Maximum speed of about Mach 1.7 (1,700 km/h) at high altitude.

  • Armament :
  • Primary: A 30mm GSh-6-30 six-barrel Gatling-style cannon, powerful for close-air support.
  • External Hardpoints: Capable of carrying up to 4,000 kg of ordnance, including bombs, rockets, and air-to-ground missiles.

  • Notable Feature : Variable-sweep wings, enabling greater maneuverability during low-level strikes and allowing for higher speed in both attack and retreat maneuvers.

  • Range : Operational range of approximately 780 km for strike missions.

  • Crew : Single-seat cockpit.

Historical Significance


The MiG-27 was significant in enhancing the IAF’s tactical strike and ground-attack capabilities. Its ability to carry heavy payloads, combined with the variable-sweep wing design, made it ideal for precision bombing in rugged terrains, including high-altitude regions. The MiG-27, produced in India by HAL, was one of the few aircraft in the IAF’s inventory that specialized in ground-attack, giving India a critical edge in offensive air capabilities.

Notable Missions


  • Kargil War (1999): The MiG-27 was one of the key aircraft used during the conflict, where it conducted high-altitude bombing missions against enemy positions on mountain ridges. Its accuracy and ability to operate in harsh, elevated environments made it crucial to India’s success in the campaign.

  • Border Patrol and Deterrence:The MiG-27 was frequently deployed for patrolling and as a show of force along India’s borders. Its ground-attack capabilities served as a powerful deterrent in high-stakes environments.

  • Counter-Insurgency and Training Missions:The MiG-27 was used in various counter-insurgency operations and for training strike pilots in low-level attack techniques, as its design was particularly suited to such roles.
