FERRET
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : The Ferret entered service with the Indian Army in the 1950s, acquired from the British as part of a post-World War II

    modernization effort. Designed as a scout car, it complemented India’s light armor capabilities and was primarily used for reconnaissance.


  • Mjaor Operation : The Ferret saw active duty in the 1965 and 1971 Indo-Pak Wars. Its role in these conflicts

    was primarily in reconnaissance and patrol operations, where its speed and mobility allowed

    it to gather intelligence and support larger armored units.


  • Retirement: By the 1980s, the Ferret had largely been phased out of active service in the Indian Army, replaced by more

    modern and better-protected armored reconnaissance vehicles. Some units were retained in non-combat roles or training until they were fully retired.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 4 tons.

  • Engine : Powered by aRolls-Royce B60 petrol engine with 129 horsepower.

  • Speed : Maximum speed of 93 km/h on-road, making it one of the faster light armored vehicles in its class.

  • Armament :
  • Primary : Typically armed with a 7.62mm machine gun, though variants could mount

    • heavier weapons or smoke dischargers for additional protection.

  • Secondary : Some models were equipped with smoke grenade launchers for defensive screening.

  • Armor : Welded steel armor, offering protection against small arms fire and shrapnel, though not sufficient against heavier anti-tank weaponry.

  • Range : Operational range of approximately 300 km.

  • Crew : Operated by a crew of two (driver and commander/gunner), reflecting its compact and nimble design.

Historical Significance


The Ferret Armored Car was significant as one of the first dedicated reconnaissance vehicles in the Indian Army.

Its lightweight design and mobility allowed it to quickly scout and gather intelligence

on enemy positions, providing crucial information that could support larger armored forces.

The Ferret’s presence in India’s armored fleet helped refine reconnaissance tactics in both peacetime and combat scenarios.


Notable Missions


  • 1965 Indo-Pak War:The Ferret was deployed for reconnaissance on the western front, where it helped gather

    intelligence on enemy movements and terrain features, supporting the Indian Army’s armored thrusts.


  • 1971 Indo-Pak War: The Ferret played a similar role in the 1971 conflict, providing valuable support to Indian forces in the desert regions of Rajasthan

    and the riverine terrain of East Pakistan (now Bangladesh). Its compact design allowed it to navigate difficult terrains and support infantry units in forward areas.
