Dassault Ouragon (Toffani)
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : The Dassault Ouragan entered service with the Indian Air Force in 1953, making it one of the IAF’s

    first-generation jet fighters. India acquired about 104 Ouragans from France, marking the beginning of a long-standing defense relationship with France.


  • Mjaor Operation : The Ouragan Toofani was active during the 1961 annexation of Goa and saw extensive action in the 1965 Indo-Pak War.

    It performed ground-attack missions, provided air cover, and played a crucial role in close support for ground troops.


  • Retirement: The Ouragan was phased out in 1975 as more advanced aircraft like the MiG-21 and the Mystère IV took over. Although limited

    in speed and armament by later standards, it served effectively for over two decades.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 4 tons (empty weight).

  • Engine : Equipped with a Hispano-Suiza Nene 104B turbojet engine, producing around 5,000 pounds of thrust.

  • Speed : Capable of reaching speeds up to Mach 0.79 (about 920 km/h).

  • Armament :
  • Primary: Four 20mm Hispano-Suiza cannons, giving it considerable firepower for its time.
  • External Hardpoints: Could carry up to 500 kg of ordnance, including bombs, rockets, and drop tanks.

  • Notable Feature : Its compact and simple design made it a low-maintenance aircraft, which was beneficial for operational readiness in the early days of jet fighters.

  • Range : Operational range of around 925 km, which could be extended with external fuel tanks.

  • Crew : Single-seat cockpit.

Historical Significance


The Ouragan Toofani played a pioneering role in introducing the IAF to jet combat operations, training, and tactics. It gave India a critical edge in regional air power during its early years in service and provided vital support in the 1961 Goa operation. It symbolized the beginning of India’s transition into a jet-equipped air force and set the stage for subsequent jet acquisitions.

Notable Missions


  • Annexation of Goa (1961): The Ouragan played a role in Operation Vijay during India’s annexation of Goa, supporting ground operations with reconnaissance and air cover over the Portuguese-controlled territory.

  • 1965 Indo-Pak War:The Ouragan saw extensive action in close air support and ground-attack roles, taking on enemy installations, supply lines, and communication networks. Its reliability and agility allowed it to perform well despite being slower than many contemporary fighters.

  • Training and Familiarization:The Ouragan Toofani was also instrumental in familiarizing IAF pilots with jet aircraft operations, becoming a training aircraft as newer models arrived.
