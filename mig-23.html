MIKOYAN-GUREVICH MiG-23
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : The MiG-23 was inducted into the Indian Air Force in 1981. India acquired two variants: the MiG-23BN for ground attack and

    the MiG-23MF for air superiority. It was the first aircraft in the IAF with a swing-wing, allowing for greater maneuverability and speed adjustments.


  • Mjaor Operation : The MiG-23 played a critical role in the Kargil War of 1999, where it provided close air support and engaged in precision bombing

    against enemy positions in mountainous terrain. It was also deployed during Operation Meghdoot to support high-altitude operations in Siachen and patrolled India’s borders during tense periods.


  • Retirement: The MiG-23MF variant was phased out in 2007, while the MiG-23BN was retired in 2009.

    Although versatile and powerful, the MiG-23 was eventually replaced by more advanced multi-role fighters like the Mirage 2000 and Sukhoi Su-30 MKI.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 10.4 tons (empty weight).

  • Engine : Powered by aTumansky R-29-300 turbojet engine,delivering up to 18,300 pounds of thrust with afterburner.

  • Speed : Maximum speed of around Mach 2.35 (2,445 km/h) at high altitude.

  • Armament :
  • Primary: A 23mm Gryazev-Shipunov GSh-23L twin-barrel cannon with 200 rounds.
  • Secondary: Up to 3,000 kg of weapons, including air-to-air missiles (like R-23 and R-60), bombs, rockets, and guided munitions for ground attack.

  • Notable Feature : Variable-sweep wings, allowing the aircraft to adjust wing angles for optimal performance in both high-speed and low-speed conditions.

  • Range : Operational range of approximately 1,500 km with drop tanks.

  • Crew : Single-seat cockpit.

Historical Significance


The MiG-23 significantly bolstered India’s tactical airstrike and air defense capabilities. Its variable-geometry wings made it suitable for high-speed engagements and low-level attack missions, especially in mountainous terrains where maneuverability and speed were crucial. The MiG-23 introduced new technological features to the IAF and played a crucial role in operations along India's northern and western borders.

Notable Missions


  • Kargil War (1999): The MiG-23 provided close air support during the Kargil conflict, where its ground-attack capabilities were

    used to destroy enemy positions on high-altitude ridges. Its precision bombing and agility were invaluable in high-altitude warfare.


  • Operation Meghdoot:The MiG-23BN was deployed in support of Operation Meghdoot in the Siachen Glacier region, where it

    supported Indian forces in the harsh, high-altitude environment. Its ability to operate in challenging conditions made it a key player in maintaining India’s dominance in the region.


  • Border Patrols and Air Defense:Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, MiG-23s patrolled India’s borders,

    particularly during periods of heightened tension with neighboring countries, where they acted as a deterrent and ensured air superiority.
