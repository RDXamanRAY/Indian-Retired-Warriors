Service History


  • Entry into service : The Centurion was itroduced to the Indian Army in the 1950s as part of a major modernization of India's armored corps .

    Developed by the British in the 1940s, it was one of the first tanks India imported to strengthen its tank fleet.


  • Mjaor Operation : The Centurion served prominently in the 1965 and 1971 Indo-Pak Wars. In the 1965 war,

    it was part of the decisive Battle of Asal Uttar , where the tank's durability and firepower were critical in halting advancing enemy forces .


  • Retirement: The Centurion continued to serve will into 1980s, though by then , the Indian Army had begun transitioning to never models like the Vijayanta and T-72 tanks.

    The Centurion was gradually retired in the early 1990s.
