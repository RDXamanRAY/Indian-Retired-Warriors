DASSAULT MYSTERE IV
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : The Dassault Mystère IV was inducted into the Indian Air Force in 1957. India purchased around

    110 aircraft, making it one of the primary jet fighters for India’s air combat fleet during that era.


  • Mjaor Operation : The Mystère IV saw significant action in the 1965 Indo-Pak War and the 1971 Indo-Pak War, where it performed ground-attack

    and close air support missions. Known for its reliability and agility, the aircraft proved effective in various offensive operations against enemy airfields, armored columns, and supply lines.


  • Retirement: The Mystère IV was retired in 1973 as newer aircraft like the MiG-21 and Sukhoi Su-7 were introduced,

    providing the IAF with more advanced speed, range, and payload capabilities.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 5 tons (empty weight).

  • Engine : Powered by a Hispano-Suiza Verdon 350 turbojet engine, delivering around 7,700 pounds of thrust.

  • Speed : Capable of reaching speeds up to Mach 0.92 (about 1,115 km/h).

  • Armament :
  • Primary: Two 30mm DEFA cannons, providing significant firepower for close air support and ground-attack roles.
  • External Hardpoints: Capable of carrying up to 900 kg of ordnance, including bombs, rockets, and external fuel tanks.

  • Notable Feature : Early supersonic capability in dives, which allowed it to escape from pursuers or engage in surprise strikes with its speed advantage.

  • Range : Operational range of approximately 925 km, extendable with drop tanks.

  • Crew : Single-seat cockpit.

Historical Significance


The Mystère IV marked India’s first entry into supersonic-era jet technology and provided the IAF with much-needed ground-attack capability. Its success in the 1965 and 1971 wars, particularly in low-level attack missions, demonstrated the Mystère’s adaptability and durability. The aircraft contributed to India’s tactical air strength and laid the groundwork for integrating advanced jet technology into the IAF.

Notable Missions


  • 1965 Indo-Pak War: The Mystère IV played a major role, striking Pakistani airfields, armored positions, and supply depots. It supported Indian ground forces with precision and agility, helping to neutralize enemy assets effectively.

  • 1971 Indo-Pak War:The Mystère IV was again deployed extensively in ground-attack missions, proving invaluable in attacking enemy fortifications and logistic lines. It contributed to the IAF’s ability to dominate the airspace over strategic regions.

  • Cross-Border Operations:The Mystère IV’s reliability and maneuverability allowed it to conduct cross-border strikes, contributing to intelligence gathering and pre-emptive strikes during times of heightened tensions with neighboring forces.
