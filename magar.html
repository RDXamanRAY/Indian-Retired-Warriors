INS MAGAR (L20)
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : INS Magar was commissioned on July 15, 1987, as the lead ship of the Magar-class landing ships, which were the first indigenously built amphibious warfare vessels of the Indian Navy, constructed by Hindustan Shipyard in Visakhapatnam.

  • Retirement : INS Magar continues to serve in the Indian Navy as of recent years, demonstrating the durability and ongoing utility of the Magar-class in India's amphibious capabilities.

Technical Specifications


  • Displacement : Approximately 5,600 tons (full load).

  • Dimensions : Length: 124.8 meters; Beam: 17.5 meters.

  • Propulsion : Powered by 2 diesel engines producing approximately 6,000 horsepower, enabling it to reach a speed of 12 knots (22 km/h).

  • Speed : Capable of reaching speeds up to 32 knots (59 km/h).

  • Armament :
  • Anti-Aircraft Guns: 2 x 40mm anti-aircraft guns for self-defense.

  • Missile Systems: Initially equipped with surface-to-air missiles (later phased out), with a focus on transport rather than offensive weaponry.

  • Capacity : Can carry approximately 500 troops and 10 tanks or equivalent armored vehicles, along with heavy equipment and supplies.

  • Landing Craft : Equipped with 4 LCVPs (Landing Craft, Vehicle, Personnel) for beach landings and troop transport.

  • Notable Feature : Designed with a large well deck that allows for the launch of small landing craft, enabling direct beach landings in amphibious assaults.

  • Range : Operational range of around 3,000 nautical miles (5,556 km) at a cruising speed of 12 knots.

  • Crew : Around 200 personnel, including officers, sailors, and operational support staff.

Historical Significance


As one of India’s first indigenously designed and built landing ships, INS Magar represents a significant milestone in India's naval construction capabilities. It embodies India’s expanding amphibious warfare abilities, allowing the Navy to project power, conduct humanitarian missions, and support ground operations during crises. INS Magar’s service has strengthened the Navy’s strategic reach, particularly in the Indian Ocean region, bolstering India’s maritime security and disaster response capabilities.

Notable Missions


  • Operation Pawan (1987-1990): Shortly after entering service, INS Magar supported India’s peacekeeping mission in Sri Lanka, transporting troops and vehicles ashore. The ship demonstrated its amphibious capabilities in a live conflict environment, proving its design's effectiveness.

  • 2004 Tsunami Relief Operations: Following the devastating 2004 tsunami, INS Magar was deployed to affected coastal regions to deliver supplies, assist with medical evacuations, and support relief efforts. This mission showcased its role in rapid-response humanitarian operations.

  • Operation Samudra Setu (2020): During the COVID-19 pandemic, INS Magar was part of the repatriation efforts for stranded Indian nationals from the Maldives, highlighting its importance in logistics, personnel transport, and support operations in peacetime.

  • Naval Exercises: INS Magar regularly participates in joint and multinational exercises with countries like the United States, Japan, and Australia, enhancing India’s amphibious operational readiness and fostering international cooperation.
