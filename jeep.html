Service History


  • Entry into service : The Jeep first entered service with the Indian Army in the 1940sintroduced by the British

    during World War II. Its lightweight and all-terrain capability made it invaluable for a range of roles, from transport to reconnaissance.


  • Mjaor Operation : The Jeep was used extensively in the 1947-48 Indo-Pak War, the 1962 Sino-Indian War, the 1965 and 1971 Indo-Pak Wars,

    and various internal security operations. Its versatility allowed it to function in various capacities,

    including as a command vehicle, reconnaissance vehicle, and light transport for troops and equipment.


  • Retirement: The original Jeep models began to be phased out in the 1980s and 1990s as more modern and specialized utility vehicles like the Maruti Gypsy and Mahindra MM series

    were introduced. However, modified Jeep variants continued to see limited service in non-combat roles even afterward.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 1,300-1,400 kg, depending on model and configuration.

  • Engine : Powered by various engines over its service life, but typically a 2.2-2.5L inline 4-cylinder petrol engineproducing around 60-70 horsepower.

  • Speed : Maximum speed of 100-110 km/h on-road, with off-road capabilities making it highly versatile.

  • Armament : Typically unarmed in its base model; however, some versions could be equipped with:

  • Light machine guns mounted for reconnaissance and patrol roles.

  • Armor : Unarmored, though modifications allowed for minimal protection in some cases.

  • Range : Operational range of around 400-450 km, depending on fuel capacity and load.

  • Crew : Standard crew of two (driver and co-driver) with capacity to carry additional personnel or cargo.

Historical Significance


The Jeep was among the first all-terrain vehicles in the Indian Army, offering unprecedented

mobility in challenging terrains, from mountains to deserts. It served as a reliable utility vehicle

that could be quickly adapted for various military roles, making it an invaluable asset in both

war and peacetime operations.
