LOCKHEED C-130 HERCULES
RETURN

Service History


  • Entry into service : India first acquired the C-130J Super Hercules variant of the Hercules family in 2011 (not the older C-130E variant).

    The IAF initially procured six C-130Js specifically configured for special operations and transport missions.


  • Mjaor Operation : The C-130J has been actively used in military and humanitarian operations, including disaster relief efforts, high-altitude landings,

    and troop deployments. It was crucial during the 2013 Uttarakhand floods and the 2015 Nepal earthquake relief operations, demonstrating its versatility and reliability.


  • Ongoing Service : The C-130J remains in active service with the IAF and continues to be utilized for tactical airlift,

    logistics, and special operations, thanks to its robust design and modern avionics.


Technical Specifications


  • Weight : Approximately 34 tons (empty weight for the C-130J variant in service).

  • Engine : Powered by four Rolls-Royce AE 2100D3 turboprop engines, each generating 4,700 horsepower.

  • Speed : Maximum speed of around 671 km/h (417 mph).

  • Armament : Generally unarmed, though it has flare dispensers and other countermeasure systems for self-defense.

  • Capacity: Able to carry approximately 19,000 kg of cargo, 92 combat troops, or 64 paratroopers.

  • Notable Feature : Equipped with advanced avionics and self-defense systems, the C-130J can conduct operations in challenging environments, including high-altitude airfields.

  • Range : Approximately 3,300 km with a full load, extendable through aerial refueling.

  • Crew : Operated by a crew of five: two pilots, a navigator, a loadmaster, and a flight engineer.

Historical Significance


The C-130J Super Hercules has provided the IAF with enhanced operational flexibility and has been instrumental in India’s ability to conduct rapid-response missions. Its high payload, range, and the ability to perform in rugged, high-altitude terrains have made it ideal for India’s strategic and tactical needs. It has significantly upgraded India’s airlift and special operations capabilities.

Notable Missions


  • 2013 Uttarakhand Flood Relief: The C-130J was used extensively in relief operations following catastrophic floods, delivering essential supplies and evacuating people from remote locations.

  • 2015 Nepal Earthquake Relief: Following the devastating earthquake, the C-130J conducted multiple sorties to deliver aid and evacuate Indian and foreign nationals, underscoring its importance in disaster relief.

  • High-Altitude Landings in Ladakh: The C-130J regularly lands at high-altitude bases in the Ladakh region, demonstrating its versatility and ability to operate in extreme conditions, especially in regions with limited infrastructure.

  • Paratrooper and Special Forces Deployment: The aircraft has been used for special operations training and missions, deploying paratroopers and Special Forces into tactical zones as needed.


The reason I provided information on the C-130J instead of the C-130E is that the Indian Air Force (IAF) only operates the C-130J Super Hercules variant and has not used the C-130E model. The C-130J was acquired to meet the IAF's requirements for a versatile and advanced tactical transport aircraft, replacing older platforms with an aircraft better suited to modern tactical and logistical demands.
